Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description:

A chocolate chip cookie is a drop cookie that features chocolate chips or chocolate morsels as its distinguishing ingredient. Chocolate chip cookies are claimed to have originated in the United States in 1938, when Ruth Graves Wakefield chopped up a Nestl√© semi-sweet chocolate bar and added the chopped chocolate to a cookie recipe; however, historical recipes for grated or chopped chocolate cookies exist prior to 1938 by various other authors.

Ingredients:

  • Salted Butter - 8 tablespoons
  • White Sugar - 1/2 cup
  • Light Brown Sugar - 1/4 cup packed
  • Vanilla - 1 teaspoon
  • Egg - 1
  • All Purpose Flour - 1 1/2 cup
  • Baking Soda - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt - 1/4 teaspoon
  • Chocolate Chips - 3/4 cup

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Soften the butter in the microwave.
  2. Combine the butter and the white and brown sugars, beat until smoothly combined. Add in the vanilla and the egg and beat until just incorporated. Be sure not to beat too long.
  3. Add in the flour, baking soda, and salt. Mix until the mixture becomes clumpy. Once the dough is able to be pressed and formed, add in the chocolate chips.
  4. Roll the dough into 12 balls and place on a baking sheet. Bake for 9-11 minutes until the cookies look puffy and dry and a light golden color.
  5. Let them cool on the pan for about 30 minutes.
